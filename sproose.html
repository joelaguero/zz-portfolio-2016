JOEL AGUERO

SPROOSE

Sproose is a webapp that lets users share and curate their personal style by following others, posting photos, and tagging items that are linked to websites, where the item can be purchased. I was contracted as the lead product designer and consultant. I used the following human-centered research and design methods in my approach:

  • Interviewing and storytelling
  • Experience diagramming
  • Storyboards
  • Information architecture
  • Thumbnail sketches
  • Lofi and hifi wireframes
  • Mock-ups

UX DESIGN

After the co-founders brought me up to speed on their vision, I used mindmaps and casual interviews to enhance my understanding of the space. I used quick sketches to flesh out key features before developing wireframes in Balsamiq for the mobile and web experiences. These relied on a feed so users could rapidly explore large amounts of content that would update in real time.

One challenge in developing an intuitive and delightful user experience involved the uploading of photos. The core value of the webapp is its ability to link user-generated content with live commerce websites, so users can easily find places to purchase items online. The backend uses public eCommerce APIs to display item information when a user hovers over a tagged object. Uploading photos required users to accurately identify the brands and types of these tagged objects; however, what would we do when there were many images of the same brand and same item? (i.e. Forever 21 cardigans) I designed a frictionless interaction flow that allowed users to input brand and item type before displaying a collection of photos, which lets users sift through many similar objects efficiently and quickly.

VISUAL DESIGN

Visually, the webapp needed to highlight the rich, colorful displays of items and apparel that we expected users to share. The white and gray theme draws attention to user content. From user interviews and testing, I learned that user's eyes are immediately drawn to the photos, de-emphasizing the comments and text until needed.

I used Photoshop and Illustrator to create these mock-ups. Images featuring people or items of clothing were pulled from the web. All other graphics, such as icons and buttons, were created by me (excluding the Sproose logo).
